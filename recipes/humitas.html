Empanadas Salteñas

Go back to the previous page

Description

A "humita salteña" is a traditional dish from the region of Salta in northern Argentina. It is a type of tamale made from a mixture of fresh corn, cheese, and spices, wrapped in corn husks and then cooked.

Ingredients

  • 18 ears of corn
  • 1/2 kg of onion
  • 1 green bell pepper
  • 1 red bell pepper
  • Green onions to taste
  • Paprika
  • Oil and salt to taste
  • 1/2 kg of country-style cheese or processed cheese

Steps

  1. Step 1

    2. Fry the onion, bell peppers, and green onions in oil with a pinch of salt and paprika for about 10 minutes.

  2. Step 2

    3. Process the corn kernels either in a blender or using a grater, and pour them into the previous mixture in the same pot.

  3. Step 3

    4. Add a level tablespoon of either fine or coarse salt. Stir until the mixture becomes paste-like, not liquid.

  4. Step 4

    5. Turn off the heat and add the cheese in cubes.

  5. Step 5

    6. Proceed to assemble the humitas once the mixture has cooled.

  6. Step 6

    7. Lastly, once the humitas are assembled, place them all together in an orderly manner in a pot and pour water until it just barely covers them, not too much. Boil for approximately 20 to 30 minutes, then they are ready to be taken out and served.
