Empanadas Salteñas

Go back to the previous page

Description

Empanadas salteñas are a traditional type of empanada that originates from Salta, a province in northern Argentina. It differs from the empanadas of other provinces because they have potatoes and meat, generally cut with a knife.They are the best empanadas in the country

Ingredients

  • 1 kilogram of meat (round, sirloin, eye of round)
  • 700 grams of onion
  • 300 grams of red bell pepper
  • 250 grams of green onion (spring onion)
  • 400 grams of potatoes, cubed
  • 200 grams of fat
  • 6 eggs
  • Salt, oregano, paprika, ground chili pepper, and cumin.

Steps

  1. Step 1

    2. Cut the onions and peppers (seedless and deveined) into evenly sized cubes.

  2. Step 2

    3. Cut the meat into small cubes and do the same with the potatoes. Blanch them for a few minutes in boiling water (they shouldn't be fully cooked).

  3. Step 3

    4. Place the fat in a pot and sauté the peppers and onions until they become translucent. Add salt.

  4. Step 4

    5. Add the meat and then the potatoes.

  5. Step 5

    6. Remove from heat a few minutes before the cooking process is complete, season, and let it cool.

  6. Step 6

    7. Add chopped green onions and chopped hard-boiled eggs just before assembling the empanadas.

  7. Step 7

    8. Roll out the dough discs until they're about 13 cm in diameter. For the dough recipe, refer to the empanada dough recipe here. (seedless and deveined) into evenly sized cubes.

  8. Step 8

    9. Bake in an oven at 250°C (482°F) for about 12 minutes or fry in ample lard.

  9. Step 9

    10. They are often served with a sauce made from crushed tomatoes, spicy chili pepper, salt, and lemon.
